# Accounts Receivable

## What is Accounts Receivable?
Accounts Receivable (AR) is the money owed to a business by its customers for goods or services delivered but not yet paid for. It is recorded as a current asset on the Balance Sheet.

## AR Process
1. **Deliver goods/services** to the customer.
2. **Issue invoice** with payment terms.
3. **Track outstanding invoices** in the AR aging report.
4. **Send reminders** for overdue accounts.
5. **Receive and record payment**.
6. **Reconcile** with bank statements.

## AR Aging Report
Categorizes outstanding invoices by how long they've been unpaid:
- 0–30 days
- 31–60 days
- 61–90 days
- 90+ days (high risk of bad debt)

## Key AR Metrics
- **Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)** = (Accounts Receivable / Net Credit Sales) × Days
- Lower DSO means faster collection — better cash flow.

## Bad Debt
When a receivable is deemed uncollectible, it is written off as bad debt expense. Businesses often maintain an **Allowance for Doubtful Accounts** to estimate expected bad debts.

## Best Practices
- Set clear payment terms upfront.
- Invoice promptly after delivery.
- Automate payment reminders.
- Offer multiple payment methods to reduce friction.
- Review the aging report weekly.

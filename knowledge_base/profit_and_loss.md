# Profit & Loss Statement (Income Statement)

## What is a Profit & Loss Statement?
A Profit & Loss (P&L) statement, also called an Income Statement, is a financial report that summarizes a company's revenues, costs, and expenses over a specific period (monthly, quarterly, or annually). It shows whether a business made a profit or incurred a loss.

## Structure of a P&L Statement

### 1. Revenue (Income)
Total money earned from selling goods or services.
- Gross Revenue / Sales
- Less: Returns and Discounts
- **Net Revenue**

### 2. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
Direct costs of producing the goods or services sold.
- Raw Materials
- Direct Labor
- Manufacturing Overheads

### 3. Gross Profit
**Gross Profit = Net Revenue - COGS**
Indicates profitability before operating expenses.

### 4. Operating Expenses (OPEX)
Indirect costs of running the business:
- Salaries and Wages
- Rent and Utilities
- Marketing and Advertising
- Depreciation
- Administrative Expenses

### 5. Operating Profit (EBIT)
**Operating Profit = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses**

### 6. Non-Operating Items
- Interest Income / Expense
- Gains or Losses on asset sales

### 7. Net Profit (Bottom Line)
**Net Profit = Operating Profit ± Non-Operating Items - Taxes**

## Why P&L Matters
- Measures business performance over time
- Helps in budgeting and forecasting
- Required for tax filing
- Used by investors and lenders to assess viability

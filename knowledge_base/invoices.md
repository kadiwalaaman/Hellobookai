# Invoices

## What is an Invoice?
An invoice is a commercial document issued by a seller to a buyer, listing goods or services provided and the amount due for payment. It serves as a legal record of a business transaction.

## Key Components of an Invoice
1. **Invoice Number**: A unique identifier for tracking purposes.
2. **Invoice Date**: The date the invoice was issued.
3. **Due Date**: When payment is expected (e.g., Net 30, Net 60).
4. **Seller Details**: Business name, address, contact information, and tax ID.
5. **Buyer Details**: Client name and billing address.
6. **Line Items**: Description, quantity, unit price, and total for each product/service.
7. **Subtotal**: Sum of all line items before tax.
8. **Taxes**: GST, VAT, or applicable sales tax.
9. **Total Amount Due**: Final amount after taxes and discounts.
10. **Payment Terms & Methods**: Bank transfer, credit card, cheque, etc.

## Types of Invoices
- **Proforma Invoice**: A preliminary invoice sent before goods/services are delivered.
- **Commercial Invoice**: Standard invoice for completed transactions.
- **Credit Note**: Issued to cancel or reduce a previous invoice.
- **Recurring Invoice**: Automatically generated for ongoing services (subscriptions).
- **Overdue Invoice**: An unpaid invoice past its due date.

## Invoice Best Practices
- Always number invoices sequentially.
- Send invoices promptly after delivering goods/services.
- Follow up on overdue invoices with reminders.
- Keep copies of all invoices for accounting and tax purposes.

## Accounts Receivable
Unpaid invoices are tracked under Accounts Receivable — money owed to your business by customers.

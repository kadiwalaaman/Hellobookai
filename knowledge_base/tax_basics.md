# Business Tax Basics

## Types of Business Taxes
1. **Income Tax**: Tax on business profits. Rate depends on business structure (sole proprietor, partnership, corporation).
2. **GST/VAT**: Goods and Services Tax or Value Added Tax — a consumption tax collected at each stage of the supply chain.
3. **Payroll Tax**: Taxes withheld from employee wages (income tax, social security, Medicare).
4. **Sales Tax**: Applied to retail sales of goods and some services (varies by region).
5. **Property Tax**: Tax on business-owned real estate or equipment.

## Tax Deductible Business Expenses
Common deductible expenses:
- Rent and utilities for business premises
- Employee salaries and benefits
- Business travel and meals (partial)
- Equipment depreciation
- Marketing and advertising
- Professional services (legal, accounting)
- Software and subscriptions

## Depreciation
The gradual expensing of a long-term asset over its useful life.
- **Straight-Line**: Equal expense each year.
- **Accelerated**: Higher expense in early years (e.g., MACRS in the US).

## Tax Filing Tips
- Keep organized records of all income and expenses throughout the year.
- Separate business and personal finances.
- Track receipts digitally using accounting software.
- Pay estimated quarterly taxes to avoid penalties.
- Work with a CPA for complex tax situations.

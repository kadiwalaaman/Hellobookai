# Bookkeeping

## What is Bookkeeping?
Bookkeeping is the systematic recording of all financial transactions made by a business. It is the foundation of accounting and ensures that financial data is organized, accurate, and up-to-date.

## Key Principles
- **Double-Entry Bookkeeping**: Every transaction affects at least two accounts. For every debit, there is a corresponding credit of the same amount.
- **Accrual vs. Cash Basis**: Cash basis records income/expenses when cash changes hands. Accrual basis records them when they are earned or incurred.
- **Consistency**: Use the same accounting methods from period to period.

## Common Bookkeeping Tasks
1. Recording daily transactions (sales, purchases, receipts, payments)
2. Reconciling bank statements
3. Managing accounts payable and receivable
4. Maintaining the general ledger
5. Processing payroll

## General Ledger
The general ledger is the master record of all financial transactions. It is organized by accounts (assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, expenses).

## Chart of Accounts
- **Assets**: Cash, Accounts Receivable, Inventory, Equipment
- **Liabilities**: Accounts Payable, Loans, Credit Cards
- **Equity**: Owner's Capital, Retained Earnings
- **Revenue**: Sales, Service Income
- **Expenses**: Rent, Salaries, Utilities, Cost of Goods Sold

# Accounts Payable

## What is Accounts Payable?
Accounts Payable (AP) refers to the short-term obligations a business owes to its suppliers or creditors for goods and services received but not yet paid for. It appears as a current liability on the Balance Sheet.

## AP Process
1. **Purchase Order (PO)**: Buyer issues a PO to the supplier.
2. **Goods/Services Receipt**: Buyer receives the goods or services.
3. **Invoice Receipt**: Supplier sends an invoice.
4. **Invoice Matching**: AP team matches PO, receipt, and invoice (3-way match).
5. **Approval**: Invoice is approved for payment.
6. **Payment**: Payment is made within agreed terms.

## Payment Terms
- **Net 30/60/90**: Payment due within 30, 60, or 90 days.
- **2/10 Net 30**: 2% discount if paid within 10 days; full amount due in 30.

## Key AP Metrics
- **Days Payable Outstanding (DPO)** = (Accounts Payable / COGS) × Days
- High DPO means the company takes longer to pay suppliers (good for cash flow, but can strain relationships).

## Best Practices
- Automate invoice processing to reduce errors.
- Take advantage of early payment discounts when cash flow allows.
- Maintain good supplier relationships by paying on time.
